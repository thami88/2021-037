Scope

  • Literature Survey

    Several approaches in implementing and developing a data-driven business intelligence platform for Smart Retail Stores currently exists. Supermarkets are increasing in demand for the increased variety of consumer products available at a single location. Currently, supermarkets are outgrowing normal retail stores in urban areas, due to the quality standards maintained by supermarket chains. Retailing mainly focuses on attracting consumers through product displays and marketing. Rygielski et al’s study on the integration of customer relationship management systems demonstrates how data mining enables automated, future-oriented analyzes. This method provides an analysis of past events offered by technologies that specialize in making decisions, such as decision support systems, as discussed in Sarker et al’s research on Expert Systems with Applications. Another CRM study on demand and supply chain planning in grocery retailing has focused on retail supply chain management and sales planning. Based on these findings, the designed system has included a component that suggests recommended promotional material for managers and notifies customers regarding promotion opportunities and a component that sends notifications to loyal customers based on their location and profile.

    Suppliers are an essential party for every supermarket chain. Supermarkets rely on suppliers to maintain the supply in relation to the customer’s demand. Wang et al. have studied a novel approach that has been trialed that enables businesses to select suppliers based on their past performance and decrease order allocation time by searching for suppliers when outsourcing. Quality-based supplier selection and, assessment based on fuzzy data research focuses on supplier evaluation, based on product quality. This system generates a fuzzy ranking mechanism to select preferred suppliers. Companies rank suppliers based on products and categorize them using the PROMETHEE evaluation system. Small retailers with low working capital can obtain profit from a decision support system, and this studied system generates seasonal product buy timing, quantity, and financial judgments. It is advised only for the use of small retailers with limited working capital. The developed system contains a component for supplier profiling, smart stock purchasing, and smart stock supply, as well as a component for locating local suppliers, based on this extensive literature review.

    Retail stores may fail to clear out high-priced and high quality luxury items and products on occasion. The venture entails a high degree of risk, and revenues fluctuate inexplicably. Qu et al. [11] propose the overview of a decision support method for retail pricing and income optimization of retail items and products, with the system being applied to offline retailing of high-priced products and items that are typically associated with high-level deviations in demand. Huber et al. [13] explore the influence of dynamic pricing in the presence of inventory factors and researchers define and discuss the possibility of establishing a classification instead of a regression problem. Findings indicate that machine learning approaches can make precise forecasts and is also suitable for large-scale demand forecasting situations. According to Wang et al. [8], Machine Learning could assist in forecasting sales for highly consumable products with a short shelf life, as it outperforms the accuracy of traditional statistical methods. The difficulties associated with Price Prediction of perishables in contemporary retail stores by forecasting demand for specific items based on previous sales data, forecasting price fluctuation based on previous price data, providing interactive visualizations of the above, and automatically extracting and summarizing competitor prices available in online stores have been included in the presented system based on these factors.

    Most studies have used strategies for predicting employee and corporate performance. It was understood that an individual's output was a primary contributor to an organization's overall effectiveness and that the organization itself was involved in success [10], [17]. Wahyuni et al. [14] studied the connection between leadership, motivation, and employee performance and how they depend on and how they affect the organization by using a multivariate method of structural equation model technique. The study examined the sales that had the most impact on organizational performance using forecasting and trend analysis, as well as decision tree algorithms and generalization linear models [4]. Sarker et al.’s study [6] on employee performance analysis uses the K-means clustering and a decision tree algorithm in the analysis. The designed system has incorporated employee and retail branch performance analysis and rating that helps the top management determine the performance of the retail organization in making management decisions more efficiently and effectively.

  • Research Gap

    Customer Relationship Management

    We found there is a lacking of supporting system for Customer Relationship Management in Sri Lankan supermarkets. Our focus develop prediction system suitable for the Sri Lankan Market

    Supplier Relationship Management

    After carefully analyzing the previous studies, we found that there was lack of attention for the supplier side management there were very few of adequate automated system for supplier and stock supply out there in the market but not intelligent and those are very expensive. Specially there were not suitable for the Sri Lankan supermarkets.

    Price & Demand Prediction

    So many previous studies developed prediction models, but no one developed the Competitive price prediction model by collecting and analyzing the other retail store's price charts, predicting price and demand in a same system and provide visualization of sales data in a same system

    Branch Performance Monitoring

    So many studies have been carried out to evaluate organizational performance, and also a variety of systems in use today to do so. But, in Sri Lanka, there are no adequate automated systems for evaluating branch-level performance of retail organizations.

  • Research Problem

    Customer Relationship Management

    1. Lack of individualized relationship management with customers.
    2. It is hard to make predictions for future promotion schemes and There is no automate promotion suggestion system.

    Supplier Relationship Management

    1. Supermarket current systems does not provide adequate recommendations to manage suppliers.
    2. Inability to decide supplier service-quality is a current problem in retail stores.
    3. When opening new branches or searching for new suppliers is a tedious task this is a current problem in retail stores.
    4. Purchasing less selling items led to surplus, extra cost and waste.

    Price & Demand Prediction

    1. Difficulty in Price and Demand Predictions of perishables in contemporary retail stores and analyzing other competitive retail store’s sales details in a proper way.

    Branch Performance Monitoring

    1. Ineffective performance measurement and management reporting of branche

  • Research Objectives

    Customer Relationship Management

    1. To develop a data-driven decision support module for Customer relationship management including recommendations for promotion scheme

    Supplier Relationship Management

    1. Objective of this to provide support for the decision-making process of the retails store management to effectively manage supplier, and stock purchases. This will make sure to provide rich system generated suggestions by eliminating human errors and save valuable time that taken for manual analyze and optimized decision-making process.

    Price & Demand Prediction

    1. Analyzing previous sales data and predict price fluctuation.
    2. Develop the Competitive price prediction model by collecting and analyzing the other retail store’s price charts.

    Branch Performance Monitoring

    1. Developed model of analyzing the branch performance
    2. Analyzing and forecasting Profit
    3. Forecasting the profit against to the customer rating
